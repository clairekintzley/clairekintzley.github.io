About Me

Hi! I’m Claire, an enthusiastic computer and data science student pursuing a Master’s in Data Science at the Colorado School of Mines, where I also recently earned my Bachelor’s in Computer Science. My passion lies at the intersection of software development and data science—bringing creative, data-driven solutions to life excites me. I’ve worked on projects spanning machine learning, spatial analysis, and time series forecasting, using tools like Python, R, and Kotlin. During my internships, I’ve tackled challenges like integrating generative AI into disaster simulations, optimizing database search performance, and automating AWS resource creation with Terraform. I love using technology to solve real-world problems, whether it’s predicting crime hotspots in Baltimore or building interactive visualizations of COVid-19 data. Beyond academics and work, I’m a varsity track and field athlete specializing in long and triple jump. Being a student-athlete has shaped my discipline, time management, and teamwork skills. I’m also deeply committed to empowering women in tech, serving in several leadership roles of the Mines ACM-W chapter and an active member of the Society of Women Engineers.
